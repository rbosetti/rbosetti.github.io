About Dr. Lazarovich

I know that entering therapy can feel scary and vulnerable. I founded Compass CBT with the intention of providing a safe and non-judgmental space for the life-changing work of therapy. I approach each client with warmth, curiosity and respect. While some people know what they want from therapy, not everyone does, and that’s okay. I collaborate with each client to identify what matters most to them and design an individualized treatment plan to alleviate suffering and enable personal growth. During our work together I welcome your feedback, and will actively monitor your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. CBT is action-oriented. We will work together to take small, comfortable steps to make larger changes in your life. This may include learning and practicing skills and strategies during sessions, reading assignments, and completing between-session exercises to translate our sessions into real, long-lasting change.

I specialize in providing individual therapy to adults whose concerns include but are not limited to:

  • Chronic worry and anxiety about health, family, relationships, work, etc..
  • Experiencing panic attacks
  • Procrastination and avoidance
  • Obsessive thinking and/or compulsions
  • Difficulty making decisions
  • Specific fears (e.g., planes, animals, heights, closed-in spaces, blood, etc..)
  • Roadblocks to personal, professional, and/or creative growth
  • Perfectionism
  • Struggling with binge eating, emotional eating, or restricting food
  • Feeling out of control with food
  • Negative body image
  • Extreme fears of being negatively judged
  • Loneliness
  • Low self-esteem
  • Self-criticism and shame
  • Struggling with assertiveness
  • Struggling with intimacy and vulnerability
  • Adjusting to a chronic illness
  • Going through a life transition
  • Struggling with understanding and managing moods and emotions
  • Struggling to connect socially or romantically
  • Struggling to find work/life balance
  • Feeling like an imposter in chosen profession
  • Ruminating about past regrets and mistakes
  • Feeling haunted by past traumatic experiences
  • Struggling with sustaining attention on work, school, or household tasks
  • Difficulty with organizing, planning, and/or following-through on tasks
  • Feeling unclear about life goals and direction
  • Feeling dissatisfied at work or in relationships
  • Feeling stressed and overwhelmed by current life circumstances

The symptoms listed above are common to many anxiety and mood disorders. Click here to learn more about my areas of expertise.

Training and Qualifications

I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in psychology from Brooklyn College and earned my M.S. and Ph.D. in clinical psychology from Hofstra University. I completed my predoctoral internship at the University of California, Davis and my postdoctoral fellowship at Cognitive Behavioral Associates, a private practice in New York City. My clinical work has spanned hospital, private practice, community health center, and university counseling center settings. Prior to founding Compass CBT, I spent five years working as a staff psychologist at Williamsburg Therapy Group in New York City. I have a comprehensive background in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT). I have taught courses on Cognitive and Behavioral Approaches to Counseling and Psychotherapy for the Mental Health Counseling Program at Brooklyn College. I utilize evidence-based practices in my clinical work, such as Exposure and Response Prevention to address anxiety disorders such as panic disorder, social anxiety, and obsessive-compulsive disorder. I approach eating and body image concerns from the Health at Every Size® (HAES) and Intuitive Eating perspectives.
