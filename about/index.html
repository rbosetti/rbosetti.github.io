About

Dr. Regina Lazarovich, Ph.D.

I know that entering therapy can feel vulnerable and scary. I founded Compass CBT with the intention of providing a safe and non-judgmental space for the challenging and life-changing work of therapy. I approach each client with warmth, curiosity, and respect. While some people know what they want from therapy, not everyone does, and that’s okay. I collaborate with each client to identify what matters most to them and design an individualized treatment plan to alleviate suffering and enable personal growth. During our work together I welcome your feedback, and will actively monitor your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I believe that the ultimate purpose of therapy is to have a positive impact on your life outside of the therapy room; if you are open to it, I will provide you with between-session assignments to translate our sessions into real, long-lasting change.

I specialize in providing individual therapy to adults whose concerns include but are not limited to:

  • Feeling unclear about life goals and direction
  • Feeling dissatisfied at work or in relationships
  • Feeling stressed and overwhelmed by current life circumstances
  • Chronic worry and anxiety about health, family, relationships, work, etc.
  • Experiencing panic attacks
  • Procrastination and avoidance
  • Struggling with obsessive thinking and/or compulsions
  • Difficulty making decisions
  • Specific fears (e.g. planes, snakes, heights, closed-in spaces, blood, etc.)
  • Wanting to remove roadblocks to personal, professional, and/or creative growth
  • Perfectionism
  • Struggling with their relationship to food
  • Negative body image
  • Extreme fears of being negatively judged.
  • Loneliness
  • Low self-esteem
  • Self-criticism and shame
  • Struggling with assertiveness
  • Struggling with intimacy and vulnerability
  • Adjusting to a chronic illness
  • Going through a life transition
  • Struggling with understanding and managing moods and emtions
  • Struggling to connect socially or romantically
  • Struggling to find work/life balance
  • Feeling like an imposter in their profession
  • Rumination about past regrets and mistakes
  • Feeling haunted by past traumatic experiences

Training and Qualifications

I received my doctorate degre in clinical psychology from Hofstra University. I completed my predoctoral internship at the University of California, Davis and my postdoctoral fellowship at Cognitive Behavioral Associates, a private practice group in New York City. My clinical work has spanned hospital, private practice, community health center, and university counseling center settings. I have a comprehensive background in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT). I have taught courses on Cognitive and Behavioral Approaches to Counseling and Psychotherapy for the Mental Health Counseling Program at Brooklyn College. I appraoch eating and body image concerns from the Health at Every Size and Intuitive Eating perspectives.
